> *Beyond the July 13th half marathon lies the real journey - transforming from a beginner runner into a disciplined athlete capable of both running strong half marathons and mastering swimming.*

## The Vision

This isn't just about running 21km on July 13th. That's merely the catalyst. The real objective is to evolve over 6 months into a disciplined, efficient athlete - someone who understands their body, breath, and the quiet rhythm that comes with consistent training.

By the end of 2025, I want to be someone who can run a strong half marathon without stopping, hold efficient breathing for 45+ minute runs, and swim 200-400m with proper form. More importantly, I want to build the foundation of an athlete - the kind of endurance that carries you in life, not just on race day.

---

## Core Philosophy

If I were coaching myself, here's what I'd prioritize:

* **Aerobic development over chasing fast times early** - Building the engine before worrying about speed
* **Breathing as a key skill, not an automatic function** - Every run is breath training
* **Never skip mobility or strength once running feels good** - The foundation prevents the fall
* **Treat swimming as a graceful craft, not sweaty cardio backup** - Technique over distance, always
* **Trust consistency > intensity, and showing up > crushing workouts** - The tortoise wins this race
* **Fall in love with quiet, repetitive long runs without distractions** - Where the real growth happens

Your journey is layered — you're not just learning to run, but becoming an athlete with trained lungs, strong legs, proper rhythm, smart recovery, and deep patience.

---

## The Phased Approach (Just a plan for now)

### Phase 1: RUNNING BASE
**Focus:** Build breathing capacity, aerobic base, and mental rhythm of a long-distance runner.

The foundation phase. Every run teaches breathing control, form awareness, and the patience that comes with aerobic development. This phase culminates with the July 13th half marathon - not as an end goal, but as proof that the base is solid.

### Phase 2: RUN + STRENGTH
**Focus:** Build power, prevent injury, maintain endurance.

Once running feels natural, we add the scaffolding. Strength training becomes non-negotiable - glute bridges, split squats, calf raises, planks, and core work. The goal isn't to become a powerlifter, but to build the structural integrity that allows for years of injury-free running.

### Phase 3: RUN + LEARN SWIMMING
**Focus:** Introduce swimming skillfully while preserving running base.

Swimming enters as an art form, not cardio. Form first, distance comes naturally. Use swimming as recovery - low joint impact while the running base maintains itself. This is where discipline really gets tested - maintaining two skills simultaneously without letting either suffer.

### Phase 4: SWIM PRIMARY + RUN MAINTENANCE
**Focus:** Develop swim endurance, use running for support.

The tables turn. Swimming becomes the primary focus while running shifts to maintenance mode. By now, running should feel like breathing - something you can do without thinking, freeing mental space to master the technical craft of swimming.

---

## Non-Negotiable Principles

* **Train only two disciplines at once** - Reduce before adding. Jack of all trades, master of none doesn't apply here.
* **Warm up and cool down every time** - No exceptions. Your future self will thank you.
* **Fuel intelligently** - Pre-run carbs, post-run protein, constant hydration. Food is fuel, not reward.
* **Mobility twice weekly minimum** - Hips, calves, hamstrings. Prevention over cure, always.
* **Deload every 4th week** - Reduce volume by 40%, let the body absorb the training. Growth happens in recovery.

## Success Metrics by Year-End

By December 2025, success looks like:
* Half marathon completed without stopping, with controlled breathing throughout
* 5K time improved by 20-25% from current baseline
* Efficient breathing maintained for 45+ minute runs
* 200-400m continuous swim with proper form and technique
* Solid strength foundation with injury-free joints
* Most importantly: The quiet confidence that comes from consistent discipline
